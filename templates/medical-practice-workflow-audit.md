## Section 2 — Inbound Communication (15 min)

> Goal: Find where patient contacts fall through the cracks.

**Phone:**
- How many inbound calls do you receive on an average day?
- What percentage do you estimate go to voicemail or are missed?
- What happens when a call is missed — is there a process, or does it depend on who notices?
- How quickly does someone call back? Is that tracked?
- **[ CALCULATE ]** Missed calls/day × average new patient value = monthly leakage estimate

**Email / Inbox:**
- Who manages the practice inbox?
- How many emails per day on average?
- What types of emails take the most time to respond to?
- Are there emails that get lost, delayed, or require escalation?
- How long does it typically take to respond to a non-urgent patient email?

**Text / Messaging:**
- Do patients text the practice? Through what platform?
- Who monitors and responds to texts?
- Are there messages that go unanswered for more than 24 hours?

**What you're listening for:** Volume gaps, response time gaps, and the phrase "it depends on who's working that day." That phrase means the process isn't systematized — it's person-dependent.

---

## Section 3 — Scheduling & Appointments (10 min)

> Goal: Quantify no-show cost and scheduling friction.

- What is your current no-show or late-cancel rate? (% estimate is fine)
- What does a missed appointment slot cost the practice on average? (Use: avg visit revenue × no-show rate × weekly visits)
- Do you currently send appointment reminders? What system? What channels?
- Do you require appointment confirmation? What percentage confirm?
- What happens when a patient doesn't confirm — is there a follow-up step?
- How are new patient appointments scheduled — online, by phone only, or both?
- How long does a new patient intake form take staff to process?

**[ CALCULATE ]** No-show rate × weekly visits × avg visit value × 52 = annual no-show revenue leak

---

## Section 4 — Patient Follow-Up & Recall (10 min)

> Goal: Find lapsed revenue that's not being recovered.

- Do you have a recall process for patients who are overdue for follow-up, annual visits, or preventive care?
- If yes — how is it currently run? Who owns it? How often?
- If no — can you estimate how many patients are overdue for a visit right now?
- Do you run any campaigns to re-engage patients who haven't visited in 12+ months?
- What is the average revenue per returning patient visit?

**[ CALCULATE ]** Lapsed patients × recovery rate estimate × avg visit value = monthly recall opportunity

---

## Section 5 — Staff Time & Manual Work (10 min)

> Goal: Convert hidden labor cost into a dollar figure.

Walk me through a typical Monday morning for your front desk staff:
- What is the first manual task they do?
- What takes the most time that feels repetitive or unnecessary?
- Are there things they do every day that you wish could "just happen automatically"?
- Is there data that gets entered in more than one place?
- Are there reports or logs that someone creates manually on a recurring basis?

**[ CALCULATE ]** Hours/week on manual tasks × hourly staff cost × 52 = annual hidden labor cost

---

## Section 6 — The Cost of Inaction (5 min)

> Goal: Make the pain tangible before presenting solutions.

- If nothing changed in the next 12 months — same missed calls, same no-shows, same inbox backlog — what would that cost you?
- Is there a specific problem that keeps coming back no matter how many times you try to fix it?
- What would it mean for your staff if [biggest pain point] was handled automatically?

**What you're listening for:** The problem they describe with the most emotional weight is the one you solve first.
